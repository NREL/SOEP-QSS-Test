EventIndicator3 Model QSS Notes

QSS needs a larger numeric differentiation time step than the 1e-6 default
to get the zero crossings accurate enough for the QSS-FMU coordination to
work. That protocol is not working reliably: investigating...

When the numeric differentiation time step causes the first zero crossing
event to occur slightly before the exact t=1 time the FMU calls appear to
show that the FMU detected the zero crossing but the discrete variable
(and thus the derivative) are not flipped to -1. The QSS-FMU zero
crossing coordination needs to be investigated/improved.

With exact math QSS2 and QSS3 don't do any requantizations since the state
trajectory is linear between zero-crossing events. In these runs the
numerical derivatives are accurate enough for no requantizations to occur.

The discrete variable is currently being tracked as a QSS variable but
with its dependencies short-circuited in the OCT-generated XML it is
never updated by QSS. Variables that will be short-circuited (discrete,
integer, boolean, ...) should not be tracked by QSS. Once the short-
circuiting design is finalized the necessary changes will be made in
QSS.
